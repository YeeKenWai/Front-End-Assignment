Islam

History in Malaysia

Islam arrived in Malaysia as early as the 12th century through trade contacts with Muslim merchants. The spread of Islam was further facilitated by the arrival of Arab traders, missionaries, and scholars. Since Islam was introduced to Malacca (now Melaka) in the 15th century, Islam had become the dominant religion in the Malay Peninsula. Today, Malaysia's official religion is Islam, and the country has a rich Islamic heritage and tradition.

Beliefs

Islam is the predominant religion in Malaysia, with the majority of Malaysians identifying as Muslims. Muslims adhere to the core beliefs of Islam, including the belief of monotheism, the belief of one God (Allah), the prophethood of Muhammad, and the teachings of the Quran as the holy book. Islam emphasizes the Five Pillars: Shahada (faith), Salah(prayer) or Salat, plural form of Salah, Zakat (charity), Sawm (fasting during Ramadan), and Hajj (pilgrimage to Mecca).

Practices

Muslims in Malaysia engage in daily prayers (Salat), which are performed five times a day in the mosque. Fasting during the month of Ramadan, giving alms (Zakat) to the needy, and performing the Hajj pilgrimage (for those who can afford it) are also important practices. Islamic customs influence various aspects of life, including dietary restrictions, modest dressing, and social etiquette.

Culture

Islamic culture in Malaysia is deeply ingrained in society, influencing language, cuisine, architecture, and social norms. Islamic arts such as calligraphy, geometric patterns, and architecture adorned with intricate designs are prevalent. Malay weddings, traditional attire (such as the Baju Kurung and Baju Melayu), and cultural practices often reflect Islamic customs and values.
