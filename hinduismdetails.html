Hinduism

History in Malaysia

Hinduism has ancient roots in Malaysia, dating back to the earliest civilizations that flourished in the region. Hindu influence reached its zenith during the Srivijaya and Majapahit empires, which were Hindu-Buddhist kingdoms. Today, Hinduism is practiced primarily by the ethnic Indian community in Malaysia.

Beliefs

Hinduism in Malaysia encompasses a diverse range of beliefs, including devotion to various deities, karma, dharma, and the cycle of rebirth. Hindus worship a multitude of gods and goddesses, with major deities including Brahma, Vishnu, Shiva, and Lakshmi.

Practices

Hindu practices in Malaysia include daily prayers, temple rituals, pilgrimage, and observance of festivals such as Deepavali (Diwali), Thaipusam, and Navaratri. Temples serve as focal points for religious and cultural activities, hosting ceremonies, weddings, and cultural performances.

Culture

Hindu culture in Malaysia is vibrant and diverse, encompassing classical dance forms such as Bharatanatyam, Carnatic music, and traditional rituals. Hindu temples are adorned with colorful sculptures, intricate carvings, and elaborate decorations, reflecting the richness of Hindu artistic traditions.
