Buddhism

History in Malaysia

Buddhism has a long history in Malaysia, dating back to ancient times with the arrival of Indian traders and missionaries. Buddhist influence in Malaysia grew during the Srivijaya and Majapahit empires. Today, Buddhism remains one of the major religions practiced by the ethnic Chinese and Indian communities in Malaysia.

Beliefs

Buddhism in Malaysia encompasses various traditions, including Theravada, Mahayana, and Vajrayana. Buddhists believe in the Four Noble Truths, the Eightfold Path, and the concept of karma and rebirth. The goal of Buddhism is to attain enlightenment (Nirvana) by following the path laid out by the Buddha.

Practices

Buddhist practices in Malaysia include meditation, chanting, offering of alms, and observance of precepts. Devotees visit temples to pay respects to the Buddha and monks, and seek spiritual guidance. Wesak Day is the celebration of Buddha's birth, enlightenment, and death, is a major festival observed by Buddhists.

Culture

Buddhist culture in Malaysia is characterized by temple architecture, traditional rituals, and artistic expressions. Temples serve as centers of religious and cultural activities, hosting festivals, ceremonies, and educational programs. Classical Buddhist art forms such as sculpture, painting, and calligraphy are valued within the community.
